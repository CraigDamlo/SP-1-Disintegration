Screen

GEN nnn
How many times the loop has passed the playhead. Each pass is one mutation of the stored buffer — it never goes backward.
Condition (PRISTINE / WORN / FADING / DYING / GONE)
Plain-English read of how far along the decay is, based on the overall decay fraction.
Waveform
A live snapshot of the loop's current shape, redrawn after every generation so you can watch it fall apart. Left and right channels decay independently and are drawn overlaid (right is the fainter trace) - watch them drift apart over time, since real tape doesn't flake evenly across both tracks.
LED meter
Fills left to right as decay progresses. Green early, amber mid-way, red near the end.

Faders (decay parameters)

Wear rate
Overall speed of aging. Higher means every generation pushes the decay further.
High‑end loss
How aggressively treble gets filtered away as the tape wears — this is what makes late generations sound muffled.
Dropout density
How much audio goes missing in small chunks, standing in for oxide flaking off the tape.
Wow & flutter
Pitch instability that grows as the loop decays — the wobble/seasick quality of worn tape.

Actions (the four buttons below the faders)

Stutter (hold)
Grabs a ~120ms slice at the current playhead position and repeats it rapidly. Release to resume normal playback exactly where it paused — a real-time glitch toy with no lasting effect on the tape.
Freeze (toggle)
Stops further decay while playback keeps running, so you can sit on whatever generation you've reached.
Bake (hold)
Temporarily blends dropped-out audio back in, like literally baking a sticky-shed tape to make it playable one more time. Release and it snaps back to the true decay state, plus a short burst of accelerated wear — the relief was borrowed, not free.
Reset tape (tap)
Reloads the last source you started from (the default pad, or your uploaded file) fresh at generation 0.

These occupy the same physical position as the real unit's four track buttons (which solo stems on the actual hardware) — repurposed here since this instrument works on one loop rather than four stems.

Side strip & rocker

Play / Pause (side strip)
Starts or stops playback. The first press also spins up the audio engine.
Fn (side strip)
Currently a visual-only shift key. Hovering shows a note about a hold-for-power gesture on the real hardware, but it isn't wired up here yet.
Rocker
On the real unit this skips tracks and, held with Fn, scrubs playback. Here it's repurposed as Reverse: flips which direction the playhead reads through the buffer.

Extras (no hardware equivalent)

Load your own loop…
Upload any audio file. It's downmixed to mono, normalized, and becomes the new pristine source tape.

Volume

Vol − / +
Steps master volume by 5%. Hold either button to repeat. Positioned at the top of the panel, matching the real unit.

Export

Record / Stop & save
Captures exactly what you're hearing in real time — every slider move, freeze, and reverse — and downloads it as a WAV when you stop.
Render … gens … Quick export
Skips listening entirely: instantly renders the chosen number of generations from the currently loaded source, using the current fader settings, and downloads the full arc as one WAV.
